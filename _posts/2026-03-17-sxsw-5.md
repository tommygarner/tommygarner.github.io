Day five had some of the most memorable case-study moments of the week. The Morgan Wallen discussion around zero-party data and lift associations was especially interesting because it showed how much richer fan understanding gets once you move past broad demographics.

---

## Session 1: Beyond the Stage: Transforming Fandom and Partnerships with Data

![IMG_5891](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/46e88306-422d-4417-97a4-ad9fa676781b)

This session made the value of zero-party data feel very real. The case study showed how direct fan responses, paired with behavioral signals, can reveal interests and affinities that standard assumptions would miss.

What stuck with me most was how practical it was. When you can connect fan identity to actual partnership strategy, "data-driven" stops being a buzzword and starts becoming a repeatable decision process.

---

## Session 2: Why Music Platforms Care About Live Music

![IMG_5893](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/90d624cf-d2cf-497c-9bb5-a25dbd20b1c7)

This panel clarified why platforms keep investing in live discovery features: live attendance and streaming behavior reinforce each other. That feedback loop makes live music a growth lever, not just an adjacent category.

I also liked the tension in the conversation around incentives and ecosystem health. It is not just about helping users find shows; it is about who benefits from the ticketing value chain and why that matters.

---

## Session 3: Keynote: Make Your Own Wave - Russ and Andreea Gleeson on Independence

This keynote was one of the strongest sessions of the week for me. Russ and Andreea framed independence as disciplined consistency over years, not a branding posture, and that made the conversation feel earned instead of motivational for its own sake.

The through-line was ownership of narrative and fan relationship. Build the body of work, keep showing up, and treat direct fan trust like infrastructure, not a campaign.

---

Day five brought together data, distribution, and artist mindset in a way that felt both strategic and deeply human.
