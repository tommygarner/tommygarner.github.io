Day three was packed, but it was also one of the most useful days for me personally. I came away with clearer career advice, better project ideas, and a stronger understanding of where the hardest problems still are.

![IMG_5871](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/0a546868-a961-4287-b6c4-4f51d50dc9f7)

---

## Session 1: How to Get the Job: Building a Career in Music

This session was very practical and honest, which I appreciated. The panelists did not pretend there is a perfect formula; they focused on communication, specificity, and follow-through. The strongest advice was simple: show people how you think before you ask for anything.

It also reinforced how relationship-driven this industry is. You can be smart and still get ignored if your outreach is generic, but thoughtful follow-up and clear intent go a long way.

---

## Session 2: Keynote: Why the Future of AI Must Be Human-Centric

![IMG_5873 (1)](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/2326eaa5-bd8d-47b7-99b4-d475bc718286)

Rana el Kaliouby made the AI conversation feel grounded instead of performative. Her framing around nonverbal communication and emotional context made me think harder about what current systems still miss, and why "human-centered" cannot just be a slogan.

I liked that she balanced optimism with standards. The keynote pushed me to ask a better question: not "is this AI impressive," but "what real value does this create for people?"

---

## Session 3: Mentor Session with Sarah Kloboves, Chartmetric

<img width="2048" height="1024" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/fb5c64c3-dc14-4b44-b9fe-b28a89db1e6f" />

My mentor conversation with Sarah was a turning point for project direction. She gave me concrete ideas tied to real industry gaps, especially around touring data and the types of analyses that actually get attention from people working in music tech.

What meant the most was getting an insider perspective on both the unresolved problems and how to make project work visible. I left that session with a clearer sense of what to build next and why it matters.

---

## Session 4: The Future of Streaming in the Superfan Era

![IMG_5875 (1)](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/54a2f034-89bb-4e4c-8cfb-babb5128d704)

This panel made the superfan conversation much more specific. Instead of treating all listeners the same, the speakers broke down how a small, highly engaged segment drives disproportionate revenue and long-term momentum.

That lens felt important for my own thinking because it links product strategy directly to fan behavior quality, not just surface-level stream counts.

---

Day three helped me connect the dots between career strategy, technical depth, and market reality in a way that felt immediately actionable.
