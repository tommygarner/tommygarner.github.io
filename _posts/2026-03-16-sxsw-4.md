Day four was a little different for me because I was driving my roommate to work, so I listened to sessions in the car and caught ideas in motion. Even in that format, one message came through clearly: this industry is smaller than it looks, and relationships compound fast.

---

## Session 1: The Future of Live Music: Immersive and Interactive

<img width="1581" height="1054" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/063bc647-be60-424b-8cb9-c96635adfc1f" />

This session pushed me to think about immersive formats less as a replacement for live shows and more as a separate product category. The most compelling ideas were the ones that did something physical concerts cannot do, instead of trying to copy the in-person experience one-to-one.

It also made me think about access differently. Lower-cost digital formats can open doors for more fans, but only if the experience still feels intentional and not like a watered-down add-on.

---

## Session 2: Reading the Room: Keys to Career Growth in the Music Business

![IMG_5857](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/cae64b02-58ba-48b1-a55e-0aec0f70bc2b)

This panel was a strong reminder that communication is not just a soft skill in music; it is core infrastructure for your career. The speakers kept coming back to timing, tone, and listening, and how quickly trust is built or lost based on small moments.

The biggest takeaway for me was relational: in a small industry, people remember how you show up. Being prepared, specific, and respectful is not optional if you want long-term credibility.
