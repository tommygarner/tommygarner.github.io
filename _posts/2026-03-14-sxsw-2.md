Day two felt very product-forward in the best way. I got to see tools in action, not just hear concepts on slides, and that made it easier to imagine what I want to build and experiment with next.

---

## Session 1: Spotify for Artists Masterclass

![IMG_5856 (1)](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/ac1fd3cc-453f-415e-adbd-4e6d3d1ea751)

The Spotify for Artists masterclass was one I had really hoped to attend, and it delivered. Seeing how the team thinks about pre-release flows, editorial pitching, fan messaging, and campaign tools made the ecosystem feel much more tangible. Instead of feeling like a black box, it felt like a set of levers artists can learn to use intentionally.

I left especially interested in how these features shape behavior over time, not just launch-week spikes. This session gave me a clearer view of what "artist strategy" looks like inside an actual platform product.

---

## Session 2: Accenture Demos and When Innovation Meets Competition: The New Era of Global Sports

![IMG_5859 (1)](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/c3ff7682-3f1a-4457-9872-7137213e4a71)

The Accenture demos and sports panel gave me a different but related lens on data infrastructure. The part I keep thinking about is pose data and athlete tracking: once movement can be captured and modeled in detail, it changes coaching, health, broadcast, and business decisions all at once.

![IMG_5862 (1)](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/596e4ef5-eddf-46ee-a2af-a9f4a627acd4)

This is the part of day two I want to experiment with most. It was a reminder that the same core pattern applies across industries: better data collection only matters if it leads to better decisions and better experiences for real people.

---

Day two pushed me to think less about "music data" versus "sports data" and more about shared product logic across both worlds.
