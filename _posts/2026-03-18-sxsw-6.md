I watched day six online because I was sick, and honestly it still landed hard. The sessions made it obvious that streaming fraud is not a side issue; it is a core trust problem that affects independent artists first and hardest.

## Session 1: Owning Your Narrative: Global Strategies for Indie Artists

This session emphasized that independent growth is less about chasing volume and more about building a coherent catalog and identity over time. The strongest examples were artists who stayed consistent, understood their audience deeply, and expanded market by market with intention.

I liked how direct the panel was about tradeoffs. Not every artist needs global superstardom, and the strategy changes a lot when the goal is a durable career instead of maximum visibility.

---

## Session 2: Streaming Fraud: Where We Are and How It's Changing

This panel was one of the most important conversations of the week. It broke down how fraud increasingly targets independent artists through fake promotion services, and how current enforcement systems can still create painful false positives.

My biggest takeaway was simple: detecting and preventing fraud earlier is a billion-dollar problem to solve, maybe bigger. The industry is improving, but the gap between fraud speed and response speed is still very real.
